## Style Prompt

Create a vivid McKinsey-caliber strategy film for `Nova FWA`, an assistive fraud, waste, and abuse risk operations platform. The visual system should feel like a polished executive briefing with real operating energy: clean white field, disciplined navy typography, premium editorial business imagery, thin cobalt rules, dynamic signal callouts, concise evidence exhibits, and understated transitions. It should feel analytical, expensive, trusted, and human, with the images carrying the business story rather than decorating static slides.

## Colors

- Canvas: `#FBFCFE` report white
- Ink: `#07172E` deep executive navy
- Secondary ink: `#4A5568` graphite annotation
- Rule: `#D8E0EA` light table rule
- Accent: `#1557C0` consulting blue
- Accent pale: `#EAF1FF` pale blue evidence block
- Signal: `#00A3A3` restrained teal signal

## Typography

- Use system sans-serif only, with tight weight discipline: 700 for titles, 600 for section labels, 400 for body.
- Avoid serif display type entirely.
- Keep letter spacing at 0 and use hierarchy through size, weight, spacing, and rules.

## Motion Rules

- Motion should resemble a consultant walking through a story: image reveal, clean text build, light table/list build, and subtle scale.
- Use image movement, signal-line reveals, focus boxes, and a closing montage to make the evidence flow feel alive while staying executive.
- No background grid, no dramatic glow, no large watermark, no blurred background, no cinematic fog.
- Entrances should be 0.35s to 0.7s, precise and understated.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not use Chinese copy.
- Use `Nova FWA` consistently as the product name.
- Do not use large serif typography or decorative watermarks.
- Do not imply automatic denial, approval, or fraud accusation.
- Do not use generic SaaS cards, oversized web UI tiles, rigid card grids, neon gradients, or theatrical blue haze.
